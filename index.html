What is it?

CDH Studio is DSDâ€™s new internship program designed for students in the IT field to reach their full potential. DSD will provide students with the necessary tools and mentors, and allow them to work independently on a project in a professional environment. This mentorship model will allow DSD to reduce integration time and therefore minimize the impact on resources. Together, DSD and the students will have the opportunity to explore and create solutions using modern technologies.

The program aims to...


  • Increase productivity
  • Attract and recruit talent
  • Increase experimentation
  • Drive innovation
  • Support the department in serving Canadians
  • Reduce integration time and cost
  • Reduce CS gender gap

DSD

what's in it for DSD?

Attract and Recruit New Talent

We must be competitive in attracting bright and young IT professionals. At Google and Facebook the average age does not exceed 29. The average age of our CS workforce is 47.6.

Students bring new ideas, new skills and new ways of collaborating that are needed to be successful in the future.

The CDH Studio program hopes to attract young talent by providing a positive student experience.

Choosing From The Best

Work terms are a great opportunity for students to learn and showcase their talents. DSD can use this opportunity to better assess student performance and potential for future hiring needs.

Spread The Word

DSD is an exciting place to work! The CDH Studio offers students an insight into the interesting work being done in DSD to support the Departmentâ€™s mandate. Let's break the stigma and show the next generation of IT professionals the modern and versatile DSD.

Experiment

We are living in a constant state of change with new technology emerging every day. The CDH Studio will be a sandbox for students to play with new technologies and an opportunity for DSD to gain insight into emerging technologies.

With this program we hope to bring more excitement, ideas, and positive change to DSD.

Community Involvement

Teams will be paired with volunteer mentors allowing students and mentors to learn and grow together. This will enable and encourage bidirectional knowledge transfer between the students and mentors.

Our Vision

V1.0

As an initial trial, CDH Studio will launch with only 3 students. The students will be provided with modern workstations, display boards, and collaboration areas to encourage team work.

The proposed timeline of the project will be as follows:

  • Pick a Project

    Students select their project on a pre-selected topic related to IT renewal

  • Project Proposal

    Students prepare a project proposal

    • describe the idea
    • divide out tasks and responsibilities
    • create project timeline
    • plan 2-week sprints

  • Code!

    Students must document work, communicate within their team, seek help, and find resources

  • Present the Project

    Students prepare a formal presentation and present their project to DSD Management.

    • answer questions
    • defend choices
    • identify lessons learned

  • More in V2.0

V2.0

Based on the success of V1.0, we hope to expand CDH Studio into multiple teams and across other divisions within CIO. We would also like to make the projects multidisciplinary with developers, business students, and UX designers working on the same team.

The final showcase will also expand to have judges that will rank the projects and one project will be selected as the winner.

CDH Studio aims to provide a valuable experience for participants and DSD.

Administration

Project Ideas

The objective of all the proposed projects are to be experimental. We want students to explore new technologies and bring their new found knowledge into the department. All projects will be stored on the DSD github account as open source projects for the word to see.

1. Project Management Tool

Using any technology of their choice, students will build and present a project management tool. The students will have to complete requirement elicitation by routinely communicating and demoing their work to the customer within DSD.


2. Chat Bot

Students will develop a chat bot. What this chat bot does will be up to the students.

3. Optional: Muse brain sensor

Students can use the development tools provided by MUSE to create a project about brain activity.

  • music playlists based on mood
  • graphic visuals based on mood
  • stress reader

4. Optional: HoloLens

Students can use the development tools provided by Microsoft to experiment with augmented reality

  • interactive game
  • 3D model of CD Howe
